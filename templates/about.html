About AuthenticAI

What does AuthenticAI do?

AuthenticAI analyses how a student writes — not what they write about. Every person has a writing fingerprint: characteristic sentence rhythms, vocabulary choices, pronoun habits, and structural patterns that stay consistent across different topics and courses. AuthenticAI builds this fingerprint from a student's submission history and alerts you when a new submission deviates discontinuously from their established pattern.

It does not compare submissions to AI output. It compares each submission to that student's own history. A flag means: this submission looks different from how this student has always written — not that it was necessarily generated by AI.

The Two Signals
Signal 1 · αQ — Assignment-Invariant

Features that reflect the writer's identity regardless of the course or topic — whether it's an essay, a lab report, or a reflection. These are the hardest traits to consciously fake because they operate below the level of deliberate style choices.

Signal 2 · αS — Course-Aware

Features that are only meaningful when comparing submissions of the same type. A lab report is expected to use more passive voice than a personal reflection — so Signal 2 only compares essays to essays, lab reports to lab reports, and so on. Signal 2 is suppressed until at least 3 submissions to the same course exist.

What do the scores mean?

Both αQ and αS are z-scores — they measure how many standard deviations a submission sits above or below that student's own historical average. A score of 0 means the submission matches the baseline exactly. A score of +2 means it is 2 standard deviations above the student's typical pattern. The detection threshold θ = {{ theta }} means a submission must deviate by at least {{ theta }} standard deviations before a flag fires.

A negative αQ (Simplification verdict) means the submission is significantly simpler than the student's baseline — also a potential integrity signal, since someone writing on behalf of a stronger student might produce unexpectedly sophisticated work, while AI paraphrasing sometimes produces unusually simple output.

⚠ A flag is not a verdict of guilt

A flag means the submission is stylistically anomalous relative to the student's established pattern. There are legitimate reasons for this — illness, unusual stress, writing in a hurry, or genuine rapid improvement. AuthenticAI surfaces anomalies for your review. The final decision always rests with you. After reviewing, use Mark Genuine or Mark Violation to record your decision and update the student's profile accordingly.
