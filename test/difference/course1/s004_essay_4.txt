I never thought I would feel this strongly about something as ordinary as how classes are delivered, but online learning has genuinely changed my relationship with studying. There was a semester last year when everything moved online and I remember sitting at my kitchen table feeling completely untethered. The lectures were there, the readings were there, but something was missing and I could not name it at the time. I think now it was the sense that someone else was also in the room paying attention. Without that, I drifted. Not dramatically, not all at once, just steadily and quietly until I realised one afternoon that I had not genuinely engaged with a single idea in three weeks. That scared me more than any grade ever has.
