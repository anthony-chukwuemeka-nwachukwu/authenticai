Online learning is harmful to student development and the evidence supports this conclusion clearly. Students who learn primarily online demonstrate weaker critical thinking, lower retention of material, and reduced capacity for sustained attention compared to students in traditional classroom environments. These deficits are not minor and they are not temporary. They compound over time. The convenience that online platforms offer is real but it is purchased at too high a price. Universities that prioritise accessibility over academic rigour are making a trade they do not fully understand. The correct response is to restrict online delivery to supplementary roles and to restore in-person instruction as the default mode of serious academic engagement.
