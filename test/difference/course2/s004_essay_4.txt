The week everything went wrong started quietly, the way those weeks usually do. I had a deadline I was not worried about, a task I kept setting aside, and a vague sense that I was on top of things. By Thursday I understood that I had been wrong about all three. What surprised me was not that I had misjudged the situation but that I had misjudged it in the same way I always do, with the same slightly too much confidence in how much time I have left. I am still not sure what changes that pattern. Probably something needs to go badly enough, often enough, for the lesson to stick somewhere deeper than where it currently sits.
