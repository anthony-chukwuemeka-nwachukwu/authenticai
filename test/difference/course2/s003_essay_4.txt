There are two things I learned from my experience with time management. First, I underestimated how much small delays accumulate. Second, I overestimated my ability to correct course without a system in place. The first lesson was uncomfortable because it required me to accept that I was less efficient than I had assumed. The second was useful because it gave me something concrete to act on. Once I introduced a basic planning structure, my output improved and the sense of falling behind diminished. The reflection was useful primarily because it produced a practical change rather than just a narrative.
