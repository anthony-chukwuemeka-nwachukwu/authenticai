Reflective practice has been identified in the literature as a core competency for the development of self-regulated learning. When time management deficits are examined through this lens, it becomes apparent that the underlying causes are frequently attributable to metacognitive gaps rather than motivational deficiencies. Students who are supported in developing structured reflection protocols demonstrate measurably improved outcomes across a range of academic and organisational tasks. It is therefore recommended that institutions incorporate regular structured reflection into their academic support frameworks, ensuring that students are equipped with the conceptual tools necessary to identify and address their own patterns of difficulty before those patterns become entrenched.
